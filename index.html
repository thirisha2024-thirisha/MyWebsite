Supply Chain Management (SCM)

Definition

Supply Chain Management (SCM) is the process of managing the flow of goods, services, information, and finances from raw materials to final delivery to customers.

Key Components

  • Planning
  • Sourcing
  • Manufacturing
  • Delivery
  • Returns

Objectives

  • Reduce costs
  • Improve efficiency
  • Enhance customer satisfaction
  • Ensure timely delivery

Advantages

  • Better inventory management
  • Improved coordination
  • Higher profit margins

Conclusion

SCM plays a vital role in modern businesses by ensuring smooth operations and customer satisfaction.
